The roof of the Saint Andre's orphanage was the one place Balthazaar could go when he needed to be away from everyone. It was fairly flat with grey slate tiles evenly spaced across its surface. None of the other children ever ventured up here as it was supposed to be off limits, a boy had fallen off the roof a few years before and died, but that never stopped Balthazaar. The orphanage itself was quite old, it was built in the 1700s, but it was still in decent shape. The three story building was down a long lane that no one ever seemed to come down. Behind the orphanage was a large stone mausaelium type building. Many of the boys and girls had tried to open the doors of the grand structure but the building's secrets remained a mystery.

  

Balthazaar idly rubbed the stump of his left wrist as he looked at the roof of the mausaelium. For some reason the stump had always itched when he thought about trying to get into the building. Balthazaar, or Bal to his friends, could never remember having a left hand. Even as a child seeing other children showing off their sigils once they appeared Bal knew he would never have one. He could still do magic, if not as strong as others since he didn't have a sigil, but that just meant he had to work harder. So at least he had that. There were others here who could do no magic at all. The sigil provided a sort of guide or affinity to what magic you were best suited to. There are even factions that would not let you in if you didn't have a sigil. The Dawn Faction being one of them. Bal had always wanted to join the Dawn Faction, not just because of what they stood for but because of the symbol on their badge, a sun rising. Something about that symbol made him feel calm, something he didn't feel often. He didn't blame the factions for their rules really, he had learnt through experience that he was not what they wanted.
